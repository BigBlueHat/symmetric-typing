    Solemak Layout

    Solemak, as you might guess, is heavily influenced by Colemak. It attempts to keep as many of the good design features of Colemak as possible, while making use of opportunities afforded by using the symmetric typing system. The S in Solemak stands for both the "Symmetric" typing system it's optimized for, and the fact that unlike Colemak, the S key does not move from its Qwerty location.

    Colemak is a well-designed, optimized layout, but for our purposes it is flawed, being designed assuming the traditional typing technique. This causes it to have made some sub-optimal choices. Most notably, the S key is moved to make way for a relocated R, which is necessary to avoid common same finger bigrams such as CR and FR. But if you use the symmetric typing system instead, there is no longer a need to relocate S. Colemak also assigns common keys H and D to relatively difficult keys in the centre of the keyboard, and of course, even you do adopt the symmetric typing system with standard Colemak, the B remains unduly awkward.

    By fixing the identified issues with Colemak, and redesigning it for compatibility with the symmetric typing philosophy, Solemak is born. It is a fully-optimized layout with a similar, low rate of same-finger bigrams as Colemak, easier to learn in many respects, (well-placed S key does not move, and only 11 keys change fingers, one fewer than Colemak), fixes some of other flaws (overuse of Colemak's centre-column keys, the frequent but awkward HE pair, and the difficult B key), and this is achieved at only the modest cost of one additional key moved in total relative to Qwerty.

    Solemak is recommended as our most optimal and comfortable layout.
