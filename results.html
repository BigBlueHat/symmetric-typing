    Alternative Layouts - Performance Comparison.

    The layouts presented in these pages (Niro, Hein, Solemak), together with some other well-known alternative layouts have been analyzed using a simple scoring algorithm. The scoring is calculated based on the frequency¹ each key is typed, with keys valued at 4 (for the easiest keys) to 0 for the most difficult. In addition a penalty is applied for the frequency¹ with which consecutive same-finger key presses occur. Although relatively simple, arguably this scoring system encapsulates the main factors for assessing keyboard layouts. If you test them against other scoring systems, let us know the results!

    The formula used for scoring is:

    score = ( F4 ) + ( 3/4 * F3 ) + ( 1/2 * F2 ) + ( 1/4 * F1 ) - (same-finger)
    ...where Fs is the frequency of keys that score s.

    This is also equivalent to:

    score = [ (best 4) + (best 8) + (best 12) + (best 18) ] / 4 - (same-finger)
    ...where (best n) refers to the frequency of a keypress being one of the n best keys.

    The scores for each key.

    Using this scoring system we evaluated our layouts, plus some other well-known layouts including Colemak, Dvorak and Workman. As well as the overall scores we also show what percentage of key presses are covered by the best 4 keys (dark blue), best 8 (+light blue), best 12 (+green) and best 18 (+yellow) keys.

    According to this analysis, Solemak is the best performing layout, but as you may expect, is the most difficult to learn of the three presented here. The layout with the best performance vs learning effort may well be Niro or Hein, due to fewer keys changing fingers.

    Of the layouts designed elsewhere, we find that Workman is marginally the best. But note it has a higher consecutive same finger ratio than any of the three layouts presented in this project, and with more keys changing relative to Qwerty, is also more difficult to learn. Colemak also performs respectably well, has the nice benefit of a low same-finger count, and is should be a little easier to learn than Workman, but is let down by not optimizing some difficult keys.

    In summary, Solemak is strongly as the overall best layout. Hein and Niro are almost as good and provide excellent performance for a reduced difficulty of learning. If you decide to try any of the layouts, give us your feedback!
