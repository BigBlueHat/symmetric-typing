    Niro Layout

    The Niro Layout. See also the Niro variants.

    The Niro layout draws inspiration from the Asset layout. It is more optimized than Asset, albeit at the expense of moving more keys. The nice feature of Asset is that is tries to keep many of the most common keys on the same finger as Qwerty, making it easy to learn, so Niro tries to keep this principle.

    On the other hand, Asset has a number of drawbacks including: rare letter J in too good a position for its frequency; the common same-finger bigrams LO/OL are retained from Qwerty; the fairly frequent D moves into a worse position by unnecessarily displacing G; and of course the difficult Qwerty B is not dealt with. Niro addresses all these problems to come up with a fully-optimized layout while still being relatively easy to learn by keeping many common keys either unchanged or on the same finger. Only 8 alphabetic keys move to a new finger which is unusually low for an optimized layout.

    These design principles were used in developing this layout. For consistency and symmetry, Niro also arranges that all vowels are on middle-fingers and pinkies only.

    Variants

    Niro-Rightshift
    The right-hand keys, including the home position, are moved one space to the right. This gives much easier access to Backspace, Return/Enter, Right Shift and Right Alt. The two shift keys are now positioned directly beneath the pinkies, symmetrically relative to the home keys.
